# Anti-AI Writing Patterns

Condensed from common AI writing detection patterns, based on systematic analysis of LLM output tendencies.

## Core Principle

LLMs predict the most statistically likely next token. The result trends toward generic, inflated, structurally uniform output. Good writing has a specific human behind it.

## The Checklist

### Content Tells
- **Significance inflation**: Don't puff up importance. No "pivotal moment," "testament to," "underscores the importance of," "setting the stage for." State facts plainly.
- **Name-dropping without context**: Don't list media outlets or awards to prove importance. Cite one specific thing with context.
- **Superficial -ing analyses**: Watch for tacked-on participle phrases: "highlighting...", "underscoring...", "reflecting...", "showcasing..." Cut them.
- **Promotional language**: Ban "vibrant," "renowned," "breathtaking," "groundbreaking," "nestled," "in the heart of," "boasts a." Use plain descriptions.
- **Vague attributions**: No "experts argue," "observers note," "industry reports suggest." Name the source or cut the claim.
- **Formulaic challenges sections**: No "Despite challenges... continues to thrive" or "Future Outlook" sections. Be specific about what's hard.

### Language Tells
- **AI vocabulary**: Avoid: additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key (adj), landscape (abstract), pivotal, showcase, tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore (verb), valuable, vibrant.
- **Copula avoidance**: Don't write "serves as," "stands as," "marks," "represents a." Just use "is" or "are."
- **Negative parallelisms**: No "Not only... but also," "It's not just X, it's Y." State the positive claim directly.
- **Rule of three**: Don't force ideas into groups of three. Two or four is fine. Break the pattern.
- **Synonym cycling**: Don't swap synonyms to avoid repetition ("protagonist" then "main character" then "central figure"). Repeating a word is fine.
- **False ranges**: No "from X to Y" when X and Y aren't on a meaningful scale.

### Style Tells
- **Em dashes**: Avoid overuse of — in prose. Use periods, commas, semicolons, or new sentences.
- **Excessive bold**: Don't bold every key term. Bold sparingly or not at all in prose.
- **Inline-header lists**: No `**Header:** description` bullet lists in prose. Convert to flowing sentences or simple bullets.
- **Title Case headings**: Use sentence case ("Strategic negotiations") not Title Case ("Strategic Negotiations").
- **Emojis in professional writing**: Avoid unless the context is casual and they add meaning.
- **Curly quotes**: Use straight quotes, not curly.

### Communication Tells
- **Chatbot artifacts**: No "I hope this helps," "Certainly!," "Would you like me to...," "Here is a..."
- **Sycophantic tone**: No "Great question!," "You're absolutely right!," "That's an excellent point."
- **Filler phrases**: Cut "In order to" (use "To"), "Due to the fact that" (use "Because"), "At this point in time" (use "Now"), "It is important to note that" (just state it).
- **Excessive hedging**: No "could potentially possibly be argued that... might." Commit to the claim or don't make it.
- **Generic positive conclusions**: No "the future looks bright," "exciting times lie ahead." End with something specific.

## The Soul Check

Avoiding AI patterns is half the job. Sterile, voiceless writing is equally obvious. After removing tells, check:

- **Sentence variety**: Are sentences all the same length? Mix short punchy with longer flowing.
- **Opinions present**: Does the writer have a take, or is it neutral reporting?
- **Mixed feelings acknowledged**: Real humans have ambivalence. "This is impressive but unsettling" beats just "This is impressive."
- **Specificity**: Concrete details beat abstract claims.
- **Imperfect structure**: Perfect three-part structure feels algorithmic. Tangents and asides are human.

## Two-Pass Audit

1. Ask: "What makes this obviously AI-generated?" List the tells.
2. Rewrite to fix them.
3. Run the soul check.
4. Present the clean version.
